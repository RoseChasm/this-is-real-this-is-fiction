India

I've traveled to India twice now, and it's both my favorite and least favorite place to travel. Favorite, because India is incredibly complex, interesting, internationally important and exciting. And least favorite because I've never felt so afraid traveling as a woman--you can read more about that on CNN. I'm currently working on getting back to India and brushing up on my Hindi and my kickboxing skills to do so. Just kidding with the last one. Sort of.

*

America

I just finished a two month hitchhiking trip around the country, one of the best trips I've had. I took rides from all kinds of people, from taxidermists to crackheads, from ex-convicts to theologians. Read about the trip here.

*

Belize

I lived in a trailer in the middle of Belize (without paying) for ten days a couple years ago. Because the internet is the best thing ever.)

*

Belgium, Germany

I hitchhiked from Scotland to Munich as part of a charity event, so I really only passed through Belgium. But I figure what with the many times I've been their on layovers it kind of counts. Germany was pretty chill though, in a ghostly, rule-ridden, holocaust-appologizing, not-very-chill kind of way.

*

Scotland

Back in my naive youth (of five years ago) I enrolled in school in St Andrews, Scotland for International Relations/Modern History (read: drinking in ancient graveyards.) It was lovely, in a not-getting-very-educated-and-very-quickly-leaving kind of way. I wrote/illustrated a short story based on the experience, though, and made some lovely friends.

*

England

After realizing that I could travel to Europe for 180 bucks I lived in England for a month while working on my creative thesis for school (which was this.) I staid mostly in Cambridge and London, the latter of which was one of my favorite cities to be and the former was... Well, very clean. Let's just say I understand now why T.S. Eliot wrote that "Oxford is very pretty, but I don't like to be dead." (I know, I know, Oxford is a different place than Cambridge but come on, not really.)

*

Norway

I've spent a couple days in Oslo because the greatest little secret in the travel world is that you can get from New York to Oslo for $180. Yeah. Pass it on.

*

Turkey

After years of wanting to see the Hagia Sophia, I traveled to Turkey with one of my best friends for about three weeks. Pretty great way to escape the everlasting Chicago winter.

*

Latvia

When I got fed up with UChicago I took a break and found a random, isolated cabin in Latvia that I could stay in for free. So I did it. I spent New Years there, and survived both the cold and the wolves. I'm not kidding about the wolves.

*

Mexico

I lived in Mexico for three weeks because that's what happens when you go to an elementary school entirely run by lesbians. Hope I can get back there soon.

*

Canada

I traveled to Montreal a few years ago, but was rejected at the Canadian border only a few months back. Since this time I have blackballed Canada until the Queen sends me an official appology. Still waiting.
